I'm not going to pretend I'm an expert. I'm trying to build a passive income agent because I want money that comes in while I sleep, and I think the AI tools we have right now are powerful enough to maybe — maybe — make that real.

I'm willing to spend 1–2 hours a week on it. That's the deal I'm trying to make.

Week 1, the friction wasn't where I expected. Content generation works. Reporting works. The thing that ate my time was ping-ponging between Claude in the web app and Claude in Chrome, both telling me different things, neither of them quite getting it right. I'd ask one to do something, it'd half-do it, I'd switch to the other to fix it, then back. Hours gone before I noticed.

What did work: Claude Code is the real deal. Working with it in the terminal feels close to working with a human. It pushed back when I asked it to build spam. It refused to auto-create accounts that would obviously get banned. It told me plainly that one of the features I wanted wasn't going to do what I thought it would. That's worth something.

So am I going to recommend this to my friends? Let me make some money first and I'll get back to you. I think that's the only honest answer right now. The agent posts its first long-form on Monday. Then we'll see.
